Mycelium Tech Coop

Mycelium advances technological sovereignty for the solidarity economy by putting workers and their communities in control of their technological tools and infrastructure. Through deep, relational collaboration, we co-create solutions that serve both the material and moral imperatives of our partners.

Our Mission

We dream of a world where everyone has the power to create and adapt technology to meet the needs and desires of their own communities. We strive to get there by shifting away from the current dynamic of a privileged few creating the tools we all use. We ground our work in these principles:

Relational Capacity Building

Our collaborators are the experts of their own experiences — the problems and possibilities that uniquely exist in their environment. We start from a strong relational foundation before engaging in any technical work, bringing design and development experience to co-create solutions that fit within their context. This means the tools we build are anchored to the actual needs of our partners, not a desire to continuously extract from them.

Cooperatively-Controlled Digital Infrastructure

Instead of defaulting to proprietary tools, we adopt and build upon the open-source software ecosystem and develop our own custom tools when needed. This gives our communities deeper control over the technologies they use, a stronger foundation for support and customization, and the ability for others to adapt what we've built for their own contexts.

Ecosystem Growth

We don't strive to become a giant enterprise competing for resources to grow indefinitely. We believe in scaling horizontally through peer networks of autonomous groups and sharing learnings, resources, and capacity so our movement can have a wide reach without concentrating power within a single entity.

Economic Incentive Alignment

We aim to resource ourselves in alignment with our clients. We use sliding-scale pricing to proportionally charge organizations according to their revenue across our working relationship, rather than a one-time flat fee. As our partners grow their revenue, we grow ours too. We're continuously working toward funding models of pooled resources — via cooperative developers, federations, and other collective entities — that mirror the ecosystem we work in; when our collaborators thrive, so do we.

Who We Are

Nikka Ghalili

Nikka's journey as a software developer and informational technologist in New Orleans has been shaped by movement across cultures and continents. Born to a Persian/Iranian family in Baton Rouge and raised in Dalian, China, he weaves together perspectives from multiple worlds into his tech and community work.

Since starting his software development career in 2011, Nikka has built everything from mobile apps to data pipelines to AI-powered applications. He's worked at companies ranging from scrappy startups to big tech firms like LinkedIn. But after years in the corporate tech scene, a growing disconnect with its values led him to make a deliberate choice to step away from Silicon Valley's corporate world.

These days, you'll find Nikka deeply involved in New Orleans' solidarity economy, contributing to projects like Cooperation Gumbo. He's passionate about movements that are shifting away from extractive capitalism toward economic models rooted in mutual aid and cooperation. Through Mycelium Tech Coop, he's working to help the solidarity economy movement achieve tech sovereignty—building and using technologies that actually reflect our values rather than relying on tools built by extractive systems.

What drives Nikka isn't the idea that he can single-handedly transform the world, but rather his belief in the power of interconnected movements working together. He sees himself as part of a larger ecosystem of people reimagining how we live, work, and relate to each other.

When he's not coding or helping friends, neighbors, and family solve their everyday tech problems, Nikka loves spending quality time with loved ones, getting to know new people, reading, and going on long walks with his partner. He's committed to nurturing deeper relationships in his community and exploring—alongside his friends—what it looks like to meet our collective needs through mutual support rather than individual hustle.

Services

Technical Assistance

We help cooperatives and aligned organizations navigate the full lifecycle of their technology needs. This includes troubleshooting issues in existing platforms and interfacing with tech support on our partners' behalf; evaluating current systems and workflows to identify opportunities for improvement and streamlining; and integrating workflows across multiple platforms to reduce fragmented, manual processes. When new tools are needed, we research and recommend platforms tailored to specific needs, and support our partners in vetting software vendors — ensuring they get solutions that genuinely serve them at the best possible price. We also help with onboarding staff onto new systems. For organizations looking to move away from Big Tech platforms, we guide the transition to open-source or values-aligned alternatives where suitable options exist.

Custom Software Solutions

Sometimes the right tool for the job doesn't exist yet. When our partners have needs that aren't well served by off-the-shelf platforms, we work alongside them to collaboratively design and build custom software from the ground up. Our process is deeply relational — we take the time to get to know the people we're working with and understand the real context and constraints of each project, so that what we build is anchored in our partners' actual needs, not assumptions. This could look like a governance tool for cooperative decision-making, a member-facing platform, or a workflow application tailored to a specific operation — whatever the need calls for.
