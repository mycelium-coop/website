OUR MISSION

<!-- @id: mission-intro -->
We dream of a world where everyone has the power to create and adapt technology to meet the needs and desires of their own communities. We strive to get there by shifting away from the current dynamic of a privileged few creating the tools we all use. We ground our work in these principles:

<!-- @id: principle-1-title -->
Relational Capacity Building

<!-- @id: principle-1-body -->
Our collaborators are the experts of their own experiences—the problems and possibilities that uniquely exist in their environment. We start from a strong relational foundation before engaging in any technical work, bringing design and development experience to co-create solutions that fit within their context. This means the tools we build are anchored to the actual needs of our partners, not a desire to continuously extract from them.

<!-- @id: principle-2-title -->
Cooperatively-Controlled Digital Infrastructure

<!-- @id: principle-2-body -->
Instead of defaulting to proprietary tools, we adopt and build upon the open-source software ecosystem and develop our own custom tools when needed. This gives our communities deeper control over the technologies they use, a stronger foundation for support and customization, and the ability for others to adapt what we've built for their own contexts.

<!-- @id: principle-3-title -->
Ecosystem Growth

<!-- @id: principle-3-body -->
We don't strive to become a giant enterprise competing for resources to grow indefinitely. We believe in scaling horizontally through peer networks of autonomous groups and sharing learnings, resources, and capacity so our movement can have a wide reach without concentrating power within a single entity.

<!-- @id: principle-4-title -->
Economic Incentive Alignment

<!-- @id: principle-4-body -->
We aim to resource ourselves in alignment with our clients. We use sliding-scale pricing to proportionally charge organizations according to their revenue across our working relationship, rather than a one-time flat fee. As our partners grow their revenue, we grow ours too. We're continuously working toward funding models of pooled resources—via cooperative developers, federations, and other collective entities—that mirror the ecosystem we work in; when our collaborators thrive, so do we.
